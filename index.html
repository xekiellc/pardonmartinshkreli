Who Is Martin Shkreli?

A Brilliant Mind the System Tried to Break

Martin Shkreli built his first hedge fund before most people finish college. He founded multiple pharmaceutical companies, pioneered rare disease drug development, and livestreamed his work with a transparency that Wall Street never expected. He was also convicted of securities fraud in 2017 — a conviction his supporters argue was driven by media hysteria, not justice.

Since his release, Shkreli has returned to pharmaceutical research, launched a drug company focused on rare diseases, and engaged openly with the public. He has shown more accountability than most executives who face far fewer consequences for far worse behavior.

A presidential pardon would not erase accountability — it would restore a man who has already paid his debt, continues to contribute, and whose prosecution was colored by one of the most relentless public vilification campaigns in modern memory.
